Why the Current Approach Wasn't WorkingDiagnosis

Three years of consistent effort without visible body composition change has a single cause: ad hoc HIIT video selection with no progressive overload. When the body is never challenged beyond what it already adapted to, it doesn't change. YouTube videos also cannot track whether you lifted heavier this week than last week.

The new plan replaces randomness with structure. Every session has specific exercises, exact weights, set/rep targets, and clear overload rules. The body will be given a reason to change.

6-Month Roadmap

A phased approach — build the habit, build the strength, then refine. Milestones are objective and measurable.

Weeks 1–4 · Phase 1
Build the Foundation
The first month is not about maximum fat loss — it's about locking in the system.
  • Learn all movements. Prioritize form over load.
  • Hit the nutrition targets 5 out of 7 days minimum.
  • Creatine taken every single day without exception.
  • Log every session: exercise, weight, sets, reps.
  • Expected weight change: 1–3 lb (mostly water from creatine loading)
  • Milestone: Can complete all exercises with correct form. Supplement routine locked in.
Weeks 5–8 · Phase 2
Progressive Overload Kicks In
Movements are automatic now. Start pushing load.
  • KB RDL: target transition to 2 × 16kg.
  • Reverse lunge: move to 2 × 15lb dumbbells.
  • Plank: target 60 seconds consistently.
  • Nordic curl: progress toward partial unsupported eccentric.
  • Visible body composition changes should be emerging.
  • Milestone: 3–4 lb fat loss. Measurable strength increases across all lifts. Clothes fitting differently.
Weeks 9–12 · Phase 3
Momentum Phase
The body is in a rhythm. This is where the aesthetic changes accelerate.
  • Target 6–8 lb total fat loss by end of Week 12.
  • Lower body development visibly apparent — glute shape and hamstring definition emerging.
  • Upper body tone visible in shoulders and arms.
  • Consider adding optional fartlek intervals to Friday run.
  • Milestone: Measurably below 22% body fat. Strength at Week 12 should significantly exceed Week 1 across every exercise.
Weeks 13–15 · Goal Window
Approach Target Body Composition
On track for 18–20% body fat at ~118–122 lb.
  • Maintain nutrition targets. Do not restrict further — TTC hormonal health matters.
  • If progress stalls for 2+ weeks: assess adherence and NEAT (step count) before adjusting calories.
  • Do not drop below 1,600 cal on any day.
  • Milestone: Goal body composition reached or within 1–2 lb.
Weeks 16–26 · Maintenance + Muscle Build
Recomposition & Longevity Phase
Goal weight achieved. Shift to body recomposition — maintain fat mass, continue building lean mass.
  • Calories move to maintenance (~2,000 cal/day).
  • Protein stays at 120–130g — muscle building continues.
  • Continue progressive overload — there is always a next level.
  • If pregnancy occurs: training continues at moderate intensity with appropriate modifications. Supplements continue (discontinue pre-workout immediately).
  • Milestone at Month 6: Strongest, leanest, and most metabolically robust version of Kelsey. Habits are automatic. System is self-sustaining.
